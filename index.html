01. About

About Me

I am a Pharmacoepidemiologist and Health Data Scientist with a PhD in Public Health (Epidemiology) from Universidade Federal da Bahia (UFBA). Currently, I serve as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Hospital Israelita Albert Einstein, contributing to Brazil's National Health Survey (EDIS-Bio/PROADI-SUS), and as a RWE Consultant at Precision Data Engineering & Data Science.

My research focuses on pharmacoepidemiology, health technology assessment (HTA), and biologic therapies — particularly for inflammatory bowel diseases, autoimmune conditions, and rare diseases. I have extensive experience designing RWE studies using administrative health databases such as DATASUS, with datasets covering 10M+ patient records across Brazil's national health system.

I am a member of the International Society for Pharmacoepidemiology (ISPE) and the Brazilian Association of Collective Health (ABRASCO).

I have worked remotely with distributed international teams for 7+ years, with advanced English and native Portuguese. I am open to collaborations in pharmacoepidemiology, HTA, and health data science.
